# Laws of O.M.A.-C.O.R.E.

*Timeless principles that should not require revision as technology evolves.*

---

### Law 1 — Every Event Has Consequences

Nothing in the world is isolated. Every event — a price movement, a news headline, a policy change, a social signal — propagates through connected systems. The consequences may be immediate or delayed, direct or indirect, large or small. But they exist.

**Implication:** The question is never whether an event has consequences. It is which consequences matter and whether they can be detected before they become obvious.

---

### Law 2 — Not Every Consequence Creates Opportunity

Most consequences are neutral, already priced, or insufficiently material to act upon. An opportunity exists only when a consequence can be translated into action that improves the system's position relative to its goals.

**Implication:** The system must distinguish between consequences worth tracking and consequences worth acting upon. These are different thresholds.

---

### Law 3 — Every Opportunity Originates from Consequences

An opportunity does not appear from nowhere. It emerges from a detectable chain: event → correlation → cluster → consequence → hypothesis → opportunity. If the chain cannot be traced, the opportunity may be illusory.

**Implication:** Every opportunity should have a traceable consequence lineage. Opportunities without lineage should be treated with suspicion.

---

### Law 4 — Knowledge Without Outcomes Is Opinion

Information that has not been tested against reality remains opinion regardless of how well-reasoned it appears. Knowledge is produced only when a hypothesis is formed, an action is taken, and the outcome is observed.

**Implication:** The system should prioritize testable hypotheses over elegant theories. An elegant theory that cannot be validated has no operational value.

---

### Law 5 — Learning Without Memory Is Impossible

If outcomes are not recorded, the system cannot learn from them. If they are recorded but not structured, the system cannot extract patterns. Memory must be persistent, structured, and queryable.

**Implication:** The quality of learning is bounded by the quality of memory. A system with poor memory cannot develop criterion, regardless of how sophisticated its models are.

---

### Law 6 — Criterion Cannot Exist Without Evidence

Criterion is not intuition. It is not a feeling. It is the accumulated weight of evidence from past hypotheses, decisions, outcomes, and corrections. Without evidence, there is no criterion — only opinion.

**Implication:** The system must track evidence as rigorously as it tracks outcomes. Evidence is the raw material from which criterion is refined.

---

### Law 7 — Complexity Must Justify Itself

Every component, every layer, every abstraction added to the system must justify its existence by improving the system's ability to detect consequences, form hypotheses, make decisions, or learn from outcomes. Complexity that does not serve these functions is debt.

**Implication:** The default answer to any proposed addition should be no. The proposer must demonstrate how it improves consequence detection, hypothesis quality, decision accuracy, or learning depth.

---

### Law 8 — Every Decision Leaves Evidence

Even decisions not to act leave evidence. The system's decision to ignore an opportunity is a decision. The reasoning that led to that decision — the threshold not met, the hypothesis too weak, the evidence insufficient — is data that should be captured.

**Implication:** The system should track decisions as carefully as outcomes. What the system chose not to do is as informative as what it chose to do.

---

### Law 9 — Everything Is Provisional

No hypothesis, no conclusion, no architectural decision, no principle in this document is final. Every claim is subject to revision when evidence accumulates against it. Certainty is the enemy of learning.

**Implication:** The system should maintain the ability to revise any of its own conclusions. A component that cannot be updated is a liability.

---

### Law 10 — No Idea Is Above Evidence

No matter how elegant, how well-reasoned, how deeply held — if the evidence contradicts it, the idea must yield. This applies to the theories in this document, the architecture of the system, the decisions of the agents, and the judgment of every component.

**Implication:** The system's ultimate authority is not its designers, its models, or its rules. It is the accumulated evidence of what works and what does not.

---

*These laws are not constraints. They are axioms — statements that the project assumes to be true until evidence proves otherwise. They should be re-evaluated periodically, but changed only when the evidence is clear.*
