# Review Protocol

*Version 1.0 — June 2026*

---

## Purpose

Every significant idea entering the Innovation Engine must be reviewed against a standard set of challenges. This protocol ensures that enthusiasm does not bypass evidence, that assumptions are surfaced, and that every idea is evaluated by the same criteria.

The protocol does not prevent ideas from being implemented. It prevents ideas from being implemented without being understood.

---

## The Fourteen Questions

Every idea review must address these questions. The reviewer may ask additional questions, but these fourteen are mandatory.

### Scope and Problem

| # | Question | Purpose |
|---|----------|---------|
| 1 | **What problem does this idea really solve?** | Distinguishes the real problem from the surface symptom. If the problem is not specific, the solution will not be specific either. |
| 2 | **Are we solving the right problem?** | Checks whether the problem is the most important one to solve right now, or whether it is a distraction from higher-priority issues. |
| 3 | **Does this improve Criterion?** | Connects the idea to the project's central mission. If it does not improve Criterion, it must have another compelling justification. |
| 4 | **Does this improve decision quality?** | Decisions are the mechanism through which Criterion produces value. If decision quality does not improve, the idea is likely tangential. |
| 5 | **Does this help transform better decisions into scarce resources?** | The strategic output of the system. If the idea does not protect or increase scarce resources, it may be consuming resources without producing them. |

### Assumptions and Evidence

| # | Question | Purpose |
|---|----------|---------|
| 6 | **What assumptions are we making without evidence?** | Surfaces unstated beliefs that may be incorrect. Every assumption is a risk. |
| 7 | **Does it simplify or complicate the system?** | Complexity is debt. If the idea adds more complexity than value, it should be simplified or rejected. |
| 8 | **Can a one-person team build, understand, and maintain it now?** | Hard constraint. If the answer is no, the idea must be deferred, simplified, or rejected. |
| 9 | **How will we know in 6 months whether this was a good decision?** | Defines the evaluation horizon. Without this, the idea cannot be validated. |
| 10 | **What could go wrong?** | Proactive risk identification. Every idea has failure modes — the question is whether they are acceptable. |

### Boundaries and Decision Rules

| # | Question | Purpose |
|---|----------|---------|
| 11 | **What must this idea NOT touch?** | Defines the safety boundary. Prevents scope creep and accidental destabilization of existing systems. |
| 12 | **What is the smallest safe version?** | Forces simplification. The smallest version that produces learning is better than the full version that may never ship. |
| 13 | **What evidence would promote it?** | Defines the path forward. If this idea works, how will we know, and what happens next? |
| 14 | **What evidence would kill it?** | Defines the exit criteria. If this idea does not work, how will we know, and when do we stop? |

---

## Verdicts

After review, the idea receives one of four verdicts:

| Verdict | Meaning | Next Action |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **IMPLEMENT NOW** | Strategic value is high, risk is controlled, validation is clear, and it supports current priorities. | Move to design and implementation on USB. |
| **STRATEGIC BACKLOG** | Valuable and aligned, but premature. Needs dependencies, evidence, or timing to be met first. | Move to strategic backlog. Review quarterly. |
| **RESEARCH LAB** | Important but unresolved. Needs experiments before design can begin. | Move to research lab. Design experiments. |
| **IDEA GRAVEYARD** | Rejected or archived due to misalignment, excessive complexity, weak evidence, or bad timing. | Move to graveyard with documented rationale. |

---

## Review Depth by Lane

| Lane | Review Depth | Who Reviews | Approver |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **GREEN** | Light review. Answer all 14 questions but can be concise. | Proposer + one reader | Proposer |
| **YELLOW** | Standard review. Full answers to all 14 questions. | Proposer + reviewer | Project lead |
| **ORANGE** | Deep review. Full answers plus architecture impact assessment. | Proposer + reviewer + architecture consultation | Project lead |
| **RED** | Maximum review. Full answers, architecture impact, operational risk assessment, contingency plan. | Proposer + reviewer + architecture + operational review | Project lead + external review if available |

---

## The Rule

No idea is sacred. Only evidence is.

Every idea, regardless of who proposed it, how exciting it seems, or how much work has already been invested, is subject to the same review protocol. Enthusiasm does not exempt an idea from challenge. Effort already spent does not justify continuing down the wrong path.
