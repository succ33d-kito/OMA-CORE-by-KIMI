# Dimension Decomposition: Expert Judgment Under Uncertainty

**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Route:** B (Focused Search)

---

## Dimensions (14 total)

### Dim 01: Military Command & Combat Decision Making
- **Angle:** How military commanders develop battlefield judgment under chaos, time pressure, and incomplete information.
- **Key questions:** How do commanders learn from combat experience? What is the role of tactical decision games (TDGs)? How does command experience change decision quality? How do they distinguish signal from noise in intelligence reports?
- **Expected sources:** Military doctrine, NDM research, Gary Klein studies, US Army/Marine Corps training manuals, RAND Corporation research.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 09 (expertise development), Dim 10 (mistakes/AARs), Dim 13 (confirmation bias).

### Dim 02: Intelligence Analysis & Structured Analytic Techniques
- **Angle:** How intelligence analysts develop judgment when evidence is ambiguous, deceptive, and incomplete.
- **Key questions:** How do analysts avoid confirmation bias? What are red teams, devil's advocacy, and ACH? How do they distinguish real signals from deception? How does experience change analytical quality?
- **Expected sources:** CIA/Heuer, UK MoD Red Teaming Guide, CNAS reports, academic studies on ACH effectiveness, intelligence community doctrine.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 11 (signal vs noise), Dim 13 (confirmation bias), Dim 12 (confidence).

### Dim 03: Professional Investors & Systematized Decision Making
- **Angle:** How elite investors (hedge funds, Dalio, etc.) develop judgment under market uncertainty.
- **Key questions:** How do investors learn from losses? How does Bridgewater systematize decisions? How do they distinguish real patterns from noise? How does confidence change over a career? How do they avoid confirmation bias?
- **Expected sources:** Bridgewater research, Dalio Principles, Soros reflexivity, Buffett/Munger philosophy, academic finance research on expert investors.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 09 (expertise), Dim 10 (mistakes), Dim 12 (confidence), Dim 14 (universal principles).

### Dim 04: Elite Surgeons & Emergency Medicine
- **Angle:** How surgeons and emergency physicians develop rapid, high-stakes judgment under uncertainty.
- **Key questions:** How do surgeons develop the "feel" that something is wrong? How do they learn from complications? How does experience change surgical decision quality? How do they avoid cognitive errors? What is the role of simulation and mentorship?
- **Expected sources:** Medical education research, Dunning-Kruger in surgery studies, emergency medicine debriefing literature, surgical simulation research, cognitive task analysis in medicine.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 05 (pilots/CRM), Dim 09 (expertise), Dim 10 (mistakes), Dim 11 (signal vs noise).

### Dim 05: Pilots, Aviation & Crew Resource Management (CRM)
- **Angle:** How pilots develop judgment and how aviation institutionalized learning from mistakes.
- **Key questions:** How did CRM evolve? How do pilots use checklists vs. intuition? How does the NTSB/AAR system work? How do pilots distinguish real emergencies from false alarms? How does confidence change over flight hours?
- **Expected sources:** FAA/NTSB reports, CRM research, Gawande (Checklist Manifesto), aviation safety databases, cockpit voice recorder analysis.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 04 (surgeons), Dim 10 (mistakes/AARs), Dim 11 (signal vs noise), Dim 13 (confirmation bias).

### Dim 06: Firefighters & First Responders Under Pressure
- **Angle:** How firefighters make split-second life-or-death decisions — the classic NDM domain.
- **Key questions:** How do firefighters develop size-up judgment? How do they learn from near-misses? What is the role of experience breadth (urban vs. rural)? How do they distinguish safe from dangerous conditions? How does confidence change?
- **Expected sources:** Gary Klein's original NDM studies, fire service training manuals, near-miss reporting systems, NDM research on first responders.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 01 (military), Dim 09 (expertise), Dim 10 (mistakes), Dim 11 (signal vs noise).

### Dim 07: Chess Grandmasters & Pattern Recognition Mastery
- **Angle:** How chess masters develop superhuman judgment in a perfect-information domain.
- **Key questions:** How do grandmasters build pattern libraries? How many chunks do they know? How does blitz vs. classical decision-making differ? How do they learn from losses? How do they distinguish strong positions from weak ones? What can this teach us about general expertise?
- **Expected sources:** Chase & Simon (chunking theory), de Groot, Ericsson chess studies, Gobet & Charness, cognitive psychology of chess expertise.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 09 (expertise), Dim 11 (signal vs noise), Dim 14 (universal principles).

### Dim 08: Scientific Research Methodology & Falsification
- **Angle:** How scientists develop judgment in the face of uncertainty — institutionalized skepticism.
- **Key questions:** How does the scientific method systematically avoid confirmation bias? How do peer review and replication work? How do experienced scientists develop research intuition? How do they distinguish real findings from noise/statistical artifacts? How does confidence in hypotheses change over time?
- **Expected sources:** Popper (falsification), Kuhn (paradigm shifts), Ioannidis (why most published research is false), recent replication crisis studies, philosophy of science.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 02 (intelligence), Dim 13 (confirmation bias), Dim 14 (universal principles).

### Dim 09: The Psychology of Expertise Development (Novice → Expert Trajectory)
- **Angle:** The universal cognitive mechanisms of how judgment develops across all domains.
- **Key questions:** What is the Dreyfus model of skill acquisition? What is deliberate practice vs. mere repetition? How does the brain physically change (neuroplasticity) with expertise? What is the role of mentorship? How long does expertise actually take? What is "fractionated expertise"?
- **Expected sources:** Ericsson, Dreyfus & Dreyfus, Kahneman & Klein (2009), cognitive neuroscience of expertise, chunking theory, implicit learning research.
- **Overlap with:** ALL dimensions.

### Dim 10: Learning from Mistakes — Debriefing, AARs, and Reflection Systems
- **Angle:** How experts transform errors into better future decisions across domains.
- **Key questions:** What are the military AAR, medical M&M, aviation ASRS, and sports debriefing systems? What makes a debrief effective vs. performative? How do experts mentally process failure? What is the role of psychological safety? How does the "pain button" concept work?
- **Expected sources:** Military AAR doctrine, medical morbidity & mortality conferences, aviation safety reporting, Amy Edmondson (psychological safety), sports psychology debriefing, Dalio's "pain + reflection = progress".
- **Overlap with:** ALL dimensions.

### Dim 11: Signal vs Noise — How Experts Filter Information
- **Angle:** The cognitive mechanisms of distinguishing relevant from irrelevant information under uncertainty.
- **Key questions:** How do experts' brains process cues differently from novices? What is thin-slicing? How does stress affect signal detection? What is the role of interoceptive awareness (Damasio)? How do experts know what to ignore? How do "weak signals" become strong?
- **Expected sources:** Signal detection theory, Damasio's somatic marker hypothesis, Klein's cue recognition research, thin-slicing studies (Gladwell/Gigerenzer), neuroscience of attention.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 02 (intelligence), Dim 06 (firefighters), Dim 07 (chess), Dim 12 (confidence).

### Dim 12: Confidence Calibration & The Dunning-Kruger Arc
- **Angle:** How confidence changes over the course of expertise development.
- **Key questions:** What is the actual (not meme) Dunning-Kruger curve? How does surgical confidence change (euphoria → humility → enlightenment → wisdom)? How do experts avoid overconfidence? What is metacognitive awareness? How do investors calibrate confidence? What is the "expertise paradox"?
- **Expected sources:** Dunning & Kruger (1999), Kruger & Dunning follow-ups, surgical confidence studies, investor overconfidence research, metacognition literature, Kahneman on illusion of validity.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 04 (surgeons), Dim 03 (investors), Dim 09 (expertise).

### Dim 13: Confirmation Bias & Institutional Debiasing Mechanisms
- **Angle:** How experts and institutions systematically avoid confirmation bias.
- **Key questions:** What is devil's advocacy? How do red teams work? What is ACH and does it work? What is pre-mortem analysis? How does "radical transparency" debias? How does peer review work as a debiasing mechanism? Why does Kahneman think institutions are better than individuals at this?
- **Expected sources:** Heuer (Psychology of Intelligence Analysis), UK MoD Red Teaming, CNAS Counterbalance, Kahneman & Klein (2009), Bridgewater culture, scientific peer review studies, AHRQ debriefing research.
- **Overlap with:** Dim 02 (intelligence), Dim 08 (science), Dim 10 (mistakes), Dim 14 (universal principles).

### Dim 14: Universal Principles & O.M.A.-C.O.R.E. Integration
- **Angle:** Cross-domain synthesis and operationalization of principles into a decision framework.
- **Key questions:** What principles are universal across all domains? Which are domain-specific? How do they relate to each other? What would an O.M.A.-C.O.R.E. decision framework look like if it incorporated these principles? What are the key mechanisms, tools, and mental models?
- **Expected sources:** All prior dimensions, integration and synthesis.
- **Overlap with:** ALL dimensions.
